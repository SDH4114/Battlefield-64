About Battlefield 64

A calm pixel chess world with anime vibes, inspired by the bright fantasy style of No Game No Life.

What is Battlefield 64?

Battlefield 64 is a minimalistic chess playground with soft pixel pieces, pastel colors and smooth animations. The board is calm and clean, but every move feels like a duel in a fantasy tournament.

The project mixes classic chess logic with a colorful game-inspired UI — glowing highlights, pixel art and a touch of “no game, no life” chaos.

Visual style

The palette is built on vivid magenta, neon pink, cyan, aqua and soft violet on top of a dark background. Gradients shift from pink to purple and from light blue to deep blue, creating a floating game-world feeling.

Chess pieces stay calm and readable from the top, while UI elements glow like arcade UI: neon borders, soft shadows and tiny particles.

Pixel & anime mood

The UI combines retro 8-bit energy with modern anime aesthetics. Smooth pixel pieces, grid layout and coordinates around the board make it feel like a tactical RPG screen.

Every selected piece wobbles slightly, as if you picked it up with your hand — small detail, big immersion.

Why this project?

Battlefield 64 is a personal playground to experiment with pixel art, chess logic, animations and UI. It’s not just a board: it’s a place to test ideas, add new themes and build custom visual stories around chess.

The plan is to grow it step by step: multiple color sets, themes, custom boards, maybe even online matches in the future.
